Meet the Mission AIs

Exoplanet exploration requires the use of AI to extend our reach further into the galaxy.

The Mission Personality™ programme has developed AI capable of more than just operation of mission payloads and systems. Trained alongside human specialists, they have the capacity to interpret data and telemetry to locally direct operations.

The Kepler Exobioform Missions are using seven bespoke AIs. Trained to have the curiosity, care and attention to detail of a human naturalist.

The AIs are individually named after famous naturalists, biologists and explorers: Acton, Bird, Darwin, Gould, Lyell, Ridgway, Wallace.

Probes have a payload of 100 drones and supporting on-planet deployment and maintenance systems. On reaching orbit, probes will also deploy a supporting cubesat network for planetary observation and connectivity. At timed intervals probes will launch a "data return mission", using a limited supply of rockets. These will carry high-definition copies of all data back to earth. An interplanetary sneakernet.

Bioform Survey Drones

Each survey drone is running one of four different image recognition algorithms designed to photograph, document and observe bioforms as they are identified.

Data on bioform type, colouration and behaviours will be transmitted immediately by the Mission Personality.

Imagery and detailed telemetry will arrive over many years.

The Algorithms

orni

Identifies and describes avian bioforms

vert

Identifies and describes vertebrate bioforms

phyt

Plant identification

biof

Bridges the gaps between the other algorithms. Looks for the unknown

Three image recognition and classification algorithms have been designed to describe and observe three main categories of bioforms: vertebrates, avians and plant life.

The algorithms have been trained on real specimens, archeological replicas and hypothetical forms created through computer-aided evolution.

The fourth algorithm is an experimental payload. It sifts the failed results of the other algorithms, attempting to find and identify new forms of life. Literally seeing the gaps between the known and the unknown.
